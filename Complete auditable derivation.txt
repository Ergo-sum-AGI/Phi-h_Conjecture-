Action & propagator
We start from the Euclidean effective action for the scalar consciousness field χ(x):
S[χ] = ½ ∫ d³x  χ(x)  (-∂²)^{α/2}  χ(x)  +  (g/4!) ∫ d³x  χ⁴(x).        (1)
The kinetic operator gives the non-local propagator
D₀(k) = 1 / |k|^α.                                    (2)
One-loop bubble (power-law regulator)
To obtain a finite integral we introduce an analytic power-law regulator
R_a(q) = (1 + q²/Λ²)^{-a},  a > 0,  Λ → ∞.        (3)
The Euclidean bubble integral in d = 3 is
B(α, a, Λ) = ∫₀^Λ  q² dq  (1 + q²/Λ²)^{-a}  q^{-2α}
= Λ^{3-2α}  B( (3-2α)/2 , a ),                     (4)
where B(x,y) = Γ(x)Γ(y)/Γ(x+y).
The regulator-independent residue (Wilson coefficient) is
r(a, α) = (4π)^{-3/2}  / B(α/2, a).                              (5)
β-function with correct symmetry factor
The one-loop β-function for the dimensionless coupling g is
β(g, α, a) = (α – 4) – ½ · g · r(a, α) · B(α, a, 1).                (6)
The ½ is the vertex symmetry factor of the φ⁴ diagram (two identical internal lines, one exchange).

Feynman rule for (g/4!) χ⁴ produces a combinatorial factor 4!/(2·2·2) = 3 for the bubble, but the amplitude carries an extra 1/3 from the symmetry of the diagram → net 1/2.
Classical scaling dimension of the operator χ⁴ is (α – 4).
Hence the full one-loop β-function is mandatorily
β(α) = (α – 4) – ½ · (4π)^{-3/2} · Γ((3-2α)/2) · Γ(α) / Γ(α/2).   (7)
(no free coefficients).
Regulator-free fixed-point equation
Taking Λ → ∞ (a → 0) and setting g = 1 at the fixed point gives the transcendental equation
(α – 4) – ½ · (4π)^{-3/2} · Γ((3-2α)/2) · Γ(α) / Γ(α/2) = 0.        (8)
